Imama Ali

Bio

I'm Imama Ali, a computer engineer/scientist with over a decade of industry experience, specializing in 2D/3D computer vision, multi-modal machine learning, and edge ML systems. I completed my PhD at the University of Memphis (2009-2013) under the supervision of Dr. Eddie L. Jacobs. At NIO, as Sr. CV Engineer I led the team that shipped and patented Sentry mode. At UtopiaCompression, as R&D Scientist/Manager, I led autonomous systems for aerial and maritime platforms in contested environments — multi-agent autonomy, passive vision-based navigation, and multi-modal intent recognition — and initiated the company's collaboration with SpaceWERX, securing a spot in the SDA TAP Lab accelerator program. Currently at Observable Space, as Computer Vision Engineer I own the end-to-end stack for a global autonomous satellite observation network: edge-deployed sky classification, rate-tracked imaging and detection for rendezvous and proximity operations (RPO), scientific camera integration on embedded Linux, and multi-site observation scheduling.
